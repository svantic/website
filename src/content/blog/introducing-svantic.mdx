Today, we're publicly launching Svantic — the platform for building AI agents that work in production.

## The Problem We Solved

Over the past two years, we watched companies try to deploy AI agents. The pattern was always the same:

- **Week 1**: Demo works. Everyone's excited.
- **Month 1**: Edge cases pile up. Reliability drops.
- **Month 3**: The "AI project" becomes a maintenance burden.
- **Month 6**: Project shelved. Team moves on.

The problem wasn't the AI. The problem was everything around it: orchestration, governance, observability, and learning from execution.

## What Svantic Is

Svantic is an **A2A-native agentic mesh** — a distributed system where every participant communicates via the Agent-to-Agent (A2A) protocol. But that's the technical description. Here's what it means in practice:

**Your applications become agents.** Any service — a CRM, a database, a browser automation tool — can register capabilities with Svantic. The AI reasons about what to do; your services execute locally.

**Capabilities execute on your infrastructure.** This is the fundamental architectural difference. In LangChain, tools run on the server. In Svantic, capabilities run on your machine. Credentials never leave your environment. Data sovereignty is built in.

**The system learns from every execution.** Our Knowledge Store captures successful patterns — navigation strategies, error recovery, authentication flows — and retrieves them for future tasks. The 10,000th document is processed faster than the 100th.

**Governance is built in, not bolted on.** Policies define what agents can do before they do it. Approval workflows route through Slack, email, or your dashboard. Every action is traceable.

## The Architecture

At the core, Svantic is a pipeline of specialized internal agents:

- **Orchestrator** classifies intent and routes to the right handler
- **Planner** designs execution strategies for complex tasks  
- **Mesh Builder** constructs agent workflows dynamically
- **Executor** runs the plan, invoking capabilities across agents
- **Critic** validates outputs before returning them
- **Learner** distills successful executions into reusable knowledge

When a user asks "Check if SKU-1234 is in stock, and if so, update the support ticket with availability," the Planner sees this requires two agents (Inventory and Support), creates a plan, and the Executor runs it — making A2A calls to each agent.

No one had to write this workflow. The AI composed it from declared capabilities.

## Deployment Models

We support three topologies:

**Standalone** — One machine, one Svantic, one or more applications. Perfect for development and single-team workloads.

**Sidecar** — One Kubernetes pod, one Svantic sidecar, one application. Each pod gets its own AI brain. 50 scraper workers means 50 autonomous agents.

**Central + Sidecar** — A central Svantic hub with distributed sidecar workers. Shared knowledge store, centralized monitoring, fleet-wide learning. What Pod A learns on Monday benefits Pod C on Tuesday.

The topologies are additive. Start standalone, move to sidecar, add central when you need fleet coordination. No code changes — only environment variables.

## Why Now

- **LLMs got capable enough.** GPT-4, Claude 3, Gemini can reason about complex multi-step tasks.
- **Costs dropped.** What cost $100 in 2023 costs $1 today.

We built Svantic because we needed it ourselves. Our team spent years building integrations with external systems. We needed agents that could adapt to change, learn from execution, and operate safely in regulated environments.
