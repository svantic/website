Today, we're publicly launching Svantic — the platform for building, orchestrating, and governing AI agents in production.

## The Problem We Saw

Over the past two years, we've watched thousands of companies try to deploy AI agents. The pattern is always the same:

1. **Week 1**: Demo works great. Everyone's excited.
2. **Month 1**: Edge cases pile up. Reliability drops.
3. **Month 3**: The "AI project" becomes a maintenance burden.
4. **Month 6**: Project shelved. Team moves on.

The problem isn't the AI. The problem is everything around it: orchestration, governance, observability, and learning.

## What We Built

Svantic is not another agent framework. It's the infrastructure layer that makes agents production-ready:

**Forge** generates production-ready agent code from OpenAPI specs, TypeScript files, or plain English. What used to take weeks now takes minutes.

**The SDK** turns any existing service into an AI-callable agent with five lines of code. Your tools run on your infrastructure — credentials never leave your environment.

**The Mesh** orchestrates multi-agent workflows dynamically. No fixed scripts. The workflow emerges from the task.

**The Knowledge Store** remembers what worked. Every successful execution is indexed. The 50,000th task is easier than the 100th.

**Guardrails** enforce policies before actions execute. Approval workflows, rate limits, data boundaries — governance built in, not bolted on.

## Why Now

Three things changed:

1. **LLMs got good enough** — GPT-4, Claude 3, Gemini can actually reason about complex tasks.
2. **Costs dropped** — What cost $100 in 2023 costs $1 today.
3. **Enterprises are ready** — Regulated industries are no longer asking "should we?" but "how do we deploy safely?"

We built Svantic because we needed it ourselves. Our team spent years building integrations with hundreds of thousands of external systems. We needed agents that could adapt to change, learn from execution, and operate safely in regulated environments.
